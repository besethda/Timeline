The Viking Age
~800 - 1050read more→
The Viking Age marks the early period of Swedish history, when Norse seafarers explored, traded, and raided across Europe. Runestones, folklore, and settlements from this era reveal the culture and influence of the Vikings.
Gustav Vasa becomes King
6th June 1523read more→
Gustav Vasa’s ascension ended the Kalmar Union and established Sweden as a sovereign nation. His rule is often seen as the beginning of modern Sweden and centralization of power.
Treaty of Roskilde
1658read more→
The Treaty of Roskilde granted Sweden substantial territorial gains, including Skåne, Halland, and Blekinge. It marked one of the largest expansions in Swedish history.
Coup, Loss of Finland, and a new constitution
1809read more→
After military defeat against Russia, Sweden lost Finland and adopted a new constitution, the Instrument of Government. This period signified major political and territorial change.
Industrialization, Emigration
1800's - 1900'sread more→
Sweden transformed from an agricultural society to an industrial one. Many Swedes emigrated to America seeking opportunities, while urban centers grew rapidly.
Universal Suffrage
1917read more→
Sweden introduced universal suffrage, granting all adult citizens the right to vote. This was a major step toward political equality and democratic governance.
Welfare expansion, economic boom
1950's - 1970'sread more→
Sweden experienced rapid economic growth, expanded healthcare and social reforms, and strengthened its welfare state, improving quality of life nationwide.
Kalmar Union Established
1397read more→
Sweden joined Denmark and Norway under a single monarch, forming the Kalmar Union. This alliance shaped Scandinavian history, influencing Sweden’s later push for independence.
Thirty years War
1630 - 1650'sread more→
Sweden expanded its influence across Europe through military campaigns, becoming a major European power. This era strengthened Sweden’s economy, army, and political presence.
The Great Northern War
1700 - 1721read more→
Sweden fought against a coalition including Russia and Denmark-Norway, resulting in the loss of its status as a dominant European power. This war reshaped the region’s balance of power.
Union with Norway
1814read more→
Following the Napoleonic Wars, Sweden entered a union with Norway. While both nations kept internal autonomy, this union influenced Scandinavian politics for nearly a century.
Dissolution of union with Norway
1905read more→
The peaceful end of the Swedish-Norwegian union marked an important step in Sweden’s modern sovereignty and national identity.
Welfare foundations and neutrality in WWI/II
1920's - 1930'sread more→
Sweden established early welfare policies while maintaining neutrality during global conflicts. These reforms laid the foundation for the modern Swedish welfare state.
